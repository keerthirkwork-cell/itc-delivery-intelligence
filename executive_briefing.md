## The Problem No One Talks About

Every IT services company has the same problem.

The weekly status call shows 18 projects as "Green." The QBR deck looks clean. The account manager says the client is happy.

Then three weeks later — the client escalates.

**The data knew. Nobody was reading it.**

---

## What This System Does

Client Delivery Intelligence is an analytics platform that monitors every client account across four dimensions simultaneously — and flags failures **before they happen.**

It answers the questions your current reporting can't:

| Question | How It's Answered |
|----------|------------------|
| Which accounts will breach SLA next week? | 4-week breach trend with early warning flag |
| Which projects are secretly failing? | Watermelon Account Detector |
| How much revenue are we leaving on the table? | Unbilled hours × billing rate model |
| Who is about to burn out? | Utilisation trend per consultant |

## The Watermelon Problem

> *"A watermelon account looks green on the outside — on-time delivery, clean status report — but is red on the inside: rising SLA breaches, falling CSAT, growing escalation volume."*

This is the most dangerous type of account because it surprises you. The client is quietly frustrated while your reports show everything is fine.

**This system detects watermelon accounts automatically** — by cross-referencing surface delivery metrics with underlying SLA and satisfaction signals.

---

## Recommendations

**🔴 Immediate**
Deploy automated SLA breach early warning. Ticket backlog growth rate of >15%/week is the strongest 14-day predictor of a breach. Alert the account manager before — not after.

**🟡 30–60 Days**
Implement the Client Health Score as a standard weekly ops report. Replace the colour-coded status deck with a data-driven score that account managers and delivery heads can act on.

**🟢 Strategic**
Connect this to your existing ITSM tool (ServiceNow / Jira) and timesheet system for real-time automated data feeds. The analysis is ready — it just needs live data piped in.
